My Story

Hello! Welcome! My name is Kenneth Cairney. I am a Writer and Archaeologist by education, a Web Developer and System Administrator by chance. It has been a long road from where I started to where I am. I am proud of what I have accomplished and I am happy with the road ahead. In youth it is difficult to decide where you want to be, but what matters is the perpetual pursuit of self-improvement. I havse been incredibly fortunate in my experiences, and the assistance and education I have received from so many incredible people is invaluable to me. Though my university studies were a pursuit of subjects I am fascinated with, it is often the case that our passions are not the most employable. The wonderful thing is that though I might not be working in Literature or Archaeology, they are always in my mind and always hobbies. The key is that regardless of where I work I will always pursue my continuing education. My recent step into SQL Analysis and System Administration has opened surprising doors and inspired me to rekindle another avenue of self-education. There is an abundance of languages and ideas to explore in the programming and development world, and this is just the first step on a new journey. I hope you enjoy my modest collection of work and check back to see my growth on this continuing journey.

Curriculum Vitae

What is your design process?

I start with a goal, what the site or page is trying to achieve. Then build a design for what the page will look like, based on that goal. Finally I work to build the design. I utilize different tools such as HTML, CSS, PHP or even Ruby to build up each unique project.

What is your average project turnaround?

It really depends on the scope of the project. If it is only a page or two, a workable version could be up inside of a week. Most projects are a bit more complicated than that and may take 2 weeks to a month to build something that works and looks professional.

Who are you clients?

So far most of my work has been based around expanding my skills, because of this clients are usually friends, colleagues or friends of friends; but in reality they can be anyone. Those wanting to set up a simple page for their personal business, or someone looking to build a more customized blog.

Why drew you to Web Development?

It has often been something I have fiddled with. Though I studied other subjects in university, none stuck with me as something I could do day-in and day-out. They were often just expanded hobbies. They gave me a strong work ethic and a lot of interesting skills, but I found the more I played around with Web Development the happier I became. The things I learned have helped me to think critically of technology, and how technology operates, pushing me to learn more and more; and grow as a person.

In what environment do you prefer to work?

While I know i can succeed in almost any environment, I prefer a workplace that fosters personal growth. I am eager to be pushed, to learn new things. So anywhere where employees can learn from each other and are encouraged to grow. A company that wants to see its employees excel.
